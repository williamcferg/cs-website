Our Philosophy

Constructionism is a learning theory developed by Seymour Papert that asserts that people learn best by using their prior knowledge to develop new knowledge while engaged in making things. This differs from instructionism, the popular classroom teaching model, which holds that knowledge is best conveyed through direct instruction via a lecture or textbook.

Constructionists at heart, we at Coding Space believe that teaching should be about providing students with building blocks, a motivating context, then getting out of their way, and being available for questions. In fact, we pride ourselves on not directly teaching students anything. To us, thatâ€™d be like giving away the punchline without delivering the joke. (If you thought math was boring in school, that's probably why!)

We believe that students should move at their own pace, and be grappling with content exactly at the right level for them, thus all Coding Space courses are self-paced. This means students do spend a lot of time working by themselves on building projects. However, students periodically have the opportunity to complete Creative Projects with a friend or two who might not necessarily be at their same level.
