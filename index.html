Why learn to code?

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Learning to code is one of the best ways to develop analytical thinking skills, such as logical thinking, mathmatical modeling, an intuition for abstraction, and familiarity with complex systems. It provides an opportunity to practice troubleshooting problems, designing creative solutions, and working in authentic team settings.

21st Century Careers

The goal of learning to code is not to become a professional programmer. However, in any career they choose, coders will find they have a superpower that will allow them to get more work done, better, and faster, through the power of automation. And students that develop a passion for software engineering will find it one of the highest rated, highest paid, and fastest growing jobs out there.
