Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Learning to code is one of the best ways to develop analytical thinking skills, such as logical thinking, mathmatical modeling, an intuition for abstraction, and familiarity with complex systems. It provides an opportunity to practice troubleshooting problems, designing creative solutions, and working in authentic team settings.

21st Century Careers

The goal of learning to code is not to become a professional programmer. In any career they choose, coders will find they have a superpower that will allow them to get more work done, better, and faster. However, students that do develop a passion for software engineering will find it one of the highest rated for job satisfaction, best paid, and fastest growing jobs markets.

Academic Advantage

Computers don't tolerate mistakes. Thus, in order to program a computer, students have to learn how to be extremely patient and precise. Surprisingly, writing elegant code has a lot in common with writing a well organized essay. These skills give students a massive leg up in their academic studies, be it rapidly grasping concepts in the classroom, figuring out tough homework problems, or confidently acing exmans and standardized tests. Coders are able to accomplish more - in academics and their extra-curriculars - through the organizational skills developed through programming.
